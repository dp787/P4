Power-Lifting Program

During this program, you will be focused on strength and not size. Judging by your one rep max, you will use different percentages and train. Unlike Power-Building, this form of lifting only focuses on strength and little size.

This is a very well put together Bench Press program. It focuses on mainly the Bench Press and putting on strength in that lift. You can use this same program for the other main lifts as well

  • Day 1:
    Bench Press/Chest and Triceps Normal-Grip Bench Press: 7 sets of 5 reps with 75% of One Rep Max;
    Lying SkullCrushers: 3 sets of 5-8 reps, weight of your choice (Better if you choose a heavy but not too heavy weight);
    Chest Dips: 4 sets of 10-15 reps (you can even add weight to yourself and do them for more fatigue, adding weight will make it more difficult and make you stronger in the long run);
    Chest Flies: 3 sets of 10-15 reps, weight of your choice (Better if you choose a heavy but not too heavy weight)

  • Day 2:
    Back and Biceps Barbell Rows Supinated Grip: 3 sets of 8-10 reps, weight of your choice (Better if you choose a heavy but not too heavy weight);
    Barbell Rows Pronated Grip: 3 sets of 8-10 reps, weight of your choice (This grip is harder than Supinated, Better if you choose a heavy but not too heavy weight);
    Barbell Curls: 3 sets of 6-8 reps (Cheat Curls, so basically make the weight heavy but not too heavy and cheat curl);
    Pull-Ups: 3 sets of 5-8 reps (Clean reps, all the way up and all the way down, focus on contracting the back. You can even make them weighted pull-ups to make it harder);
    Dumbbell Hammer Curls: 3 sets of 10-15 reps, weight of your choice

  • Day 3:
    Bench Press/Legs Close-Grip Bench Press: 4 sets of 4 reps with 80% of One Rep Max;
    Incline Bench Press: 3 sets of 5 reps, weight of your choice (Better if you choose a heavy but not too heavy weight);
    Barbell Squats: 5 sets of 5 reps, weight of your choice (Better if you choose a heavy but not too heavy weight);
    Leg Extensions: 3 sets of 10-15 reps (Focus on squeezing your legs at the top);
    Calve Raises: 3 sets of 20 reps (Don't neglect calves please. Make them weighted too)

  • Day 4:
    Shoulders Overhead Press: Work up to one clean rep (Every week);
    Overhead Press Part 2: Put a light weight on the bar and do 1 and 1/2 reps for as many reps as you can;
    Seated Side Lateral Raises: 3 sets of 8 reps, and then cheat the rest for the burn;
    Rear Delt Flies: 3 sets of 10 reps, weight of your choice (Better if you choose a LIGHT weight)

  • Day 5:
    Bench Press/Chest and Triceps and Back Normal-Grip Bench Press: 10 sets of 3 reps with 85% of your One Rep Max;
    Lying SkullCrushers: 3 sets of 5-8 reps, weight of your choice (Better if you choose a heavy but not too heavy weight);
    Chest Dips: 4 sets of 10-15 reps (you can even add weight to yourself and do them for more fatigue, adding weight will make it more difficult and make you stronger in the long run);
    Pull-Ups: 3 sets of 5-8 reps (Clean reps, all the way up and all the way down, focus on contracting the back. You can even make them weighted pull-ups to make it harder)

  • Day 6: Rest

  • Day 7: Rest

The main lifts and accessories are listed above. As you can see there is mainly a lot of sets on the main lifts. This is something called "Volume." Volume is important when trying to put on strength.


Now comes rest period between sets:

You should go for roughly 3-5 minutes rest between sets, (ONLY ON THE MAIN LIFTS SUCH AS BENCH PRESS, SQUATS and ROWS) everything else go for 45 seconds - 2 minutes between sets.
